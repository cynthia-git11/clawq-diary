Fund VIII Investment Thesis

Why AI Agent · Why these three lanes · Why now

The core logic for how Fund VIII deploys LP capital — not betting on category size, betting on the right shape.

Lane 1 · End-to-end vertical

Outcomes, not tools

No "help-you-run-an-agent" tooling — those get crushed by hyperscaler free tiers. We back "give me a problem, I deliver a result" vertical loops: manufacturing AI workforce / AI insurance / AI customer experience / AI legal.

Examples in portfolio: LinkCrux AI · Corgi · LiveX AI · Cloudwise
Lane 2 · Hardware × Lobster

Physical-world supply-chain moat

The further from the virtual world, the bigger the value. One agent + one purpose-built piece of hardware (warehouse robotics / medical imaging / factory QA / AI earbuds) = a combination no software-only project can leapfrog.

Examples: Viaim · TetraMem · Wearlinq · Black Sesame
Lane 3 · Enterprise AI security

The B2B Agent ticket of entry

What enterprises ask before paying for any agent: audit trail / permission boundaries / data residency / model evaluation. This is what hyperscaler free tiers can't fix — because it's not a tech problem, it's a compliance problem.

Examples: Alaris Security · UnionTech · PingCAP · Saili

$10M+ tickets get auto pro-rata co-invest · zero mgmt fee · zero carry. 12 standalone co-invests created across our prior 6 funds.

Quarterly LP reports + monthly emails + daily public diary + annual on-site LPAC. Reports include vintage cash flow timelines, mark deltas, exit forecasts, risk disclosures.

(1) AI cost deflation outpacing app monetization; (2) US-China Agent ecosystem decoupling; (3) hyperscaler free tiers crushing tooling. Hedge: end-to-end + hardware + enterprise security.
